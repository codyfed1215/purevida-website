We don't throw stones at the big guys. Ever.

Running any business is hard. Running a large one? I can't even imagine. The bigger you get, the more every problem amplifies. You have to standardize to survive it. You always eventually end up with very little (but not zero) bad actors in your workforce and customer base, and the only way to deal with them at scale is to write more rules. But rules don't know the difference between the good, the bad, and the indifferent. They get applied to everyone.

That's where something starts to break.

When every rule applies to every person, great customers and great employees start to blur together with the ones causing the problems. They stop being individuals. They become an amorphous blob. And the people managing the blob start to matter more than the blob itself. The script flips. Suddenly the folks on top running the show outrank the folks on the ground actually doing the work.

That's the moment customer service dies.

## Who's the Star of the Show?

At PureVida Water, we think about it in one frame: the star of the show is anyone directly supporting a customer. Everyone else is supporting cast. Doesn't matter your title, your tenure, or your office. If you're not in front of or speaking directly to a customer, your job is to make the life of the person who is in front of a customer easier.

Our mission statement doesn't mention water.

It doesn't mention customers either.

**Our mission is to be a positive influence on our employees and their families.**

That might sound backwards for a company that installs and services bottle-less water, ice, and coffee systems across DFW, Austin, and San Antonio. It's not. It's the whole point.

If we take care of the people who take care of our customers — our service guys, our sales consultants, our admin team — then everything else flows downhill. Better installs. Faster service calls. Real relationships with the Office Managers, Facilities leads, and owners at the 1,100+ Texas businesses we serve. No blob. Just people doing right by other people.

## Why This Matters If You're Shopping for an Office Water Service

When you're evaluating bottle-less water providers in Texas, you'll hear the same pitches: cleaner water, better price, no more jugs. All of that is table stakes.

The thing that actually decides whether your system gets serviced on time and with pride and care is how the provider treats the person servicing it.

Ask that question when you shop. It's the one that matters.
